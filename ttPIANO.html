5 Common Progressions  link

C - F - G (I - IV - V) [#2]
Foundation of rock and pop. Roots back to the blues.
Playing these 3 chords in different variations > other progressions - e.g. 12 bar blues: I - I - I - I - IV - IV - I - I - V - IV - I - V.
Another example is I - IV - V - IV.
Sample: I - IV - V - V (4 bars)
 Mine = I - I - IV - IV - I - I - V - V (8 bars).

C - G - Am - F (I - V - vi - IV) [#1]    vi = minor 6th
Most popular chord progression (1-5-6-4) in Western popular music.
e.g. - 'Let It Be'
Sample: (I - V - vi - IV) X2 > 8b

C - Am - F - G (I - vi - IV - V) [#3]    vi = minor 6th
Also known as the 1950s progression (doo-wop).
e.g. - 'Stand By Me'
Sample: (I - vi - IV - V) X2 > 8b > I

Am - F - C - G, vi - IV - I - V [#4]    vi = minor 6th
Darker and sadder than the others.
Sample: (vi - IV - I - V) X2 > 8b > vi

C - F - Am - G, I - IV - vi - V [#5]    vi = minor 6th
Similar to # 4 (I & vi switched) -> similar sounding progression, just not as sad.
Sample: (I - IV - vi - V) X2 > 8b > I

Usually, songs use a combination of different progressions for the different sections.
Learn the 4 chords in the five most common keys (C, A, G, E, D). Start with the key of G > A, C, D, and finally, E.
