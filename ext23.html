wide-arc [simplify it!]    golf.com * (June 24/23)
  • straight arms (no elbow bend) until shaft || to ground both going back & coming thru
    The less the elbows fold, the farther the handle of the club will be away from the body, i.e. the wider the arc of the swing. Swing club back to shaft parallel to ground with no fold in elbow and wrist. Completing backswing should feel bigger, wider, more stretched and certainly powerful.
    For the through swing side, reverse the course.
  • light ('fingers') strong grip
  • chest > fwd 1st

Take practice swing stopping where rear forarm is pointed away from you and arm has not yet bent to 90 degrees. Forarm should point back away from you rather than up to the sky. It's your back arm that pulls in or collapses and causes your front arm to fold or break down.
