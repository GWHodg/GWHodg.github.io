What is bridge?

Top 5 cards (AKQJ10) = honors. Rest = spots.
Can't follow suit (out) & play diff suit that can't win = discard.
Some deals > trump suit (outranks other suits; played only if out of lead suit) [= 'ruff'].
Defence communicates thru cards they play!
Bidding: Auction (> right to be declarer & pick trump suit; last bid) > contract (> 'level' & 'strain'/denomination):
 1♥ = have at least 5 hearts.
 1 NT = above avg hand w/ = cards ea suit.
Hand evaluation: assess trick-taking potential.

Taking Tricks
■ Count winners in ea suit (sure tricks) [p11]:
 A+K+Q=3 (no A = 0 winners).
■ overtricks: # of tricks above those req for contract.
■ long side (hand w/ more cards in given suit)
● - can't take more tricks in suit than long side has cards (p45)
● - # tricks can take ONLY limited by lgth of 'long side'! (p51)
■ 'stopper': prev opp taking ++ tricks (in NT) (Axx, KQx, QJ10) [p65]
■ blocked suits (pp 75/84/111)
● - no way to take all tricks w/o > side suit (to get back to partner/dummy)
■ play 'high cards' fr 'short side' 1st (to prevent suit being 'blocked') [p79]
■ . . .
■ entries (eg 'to dummy'): card that lets you TRANSFER LEAD fr 1 hand to other
 - high card in OTHER hand that you want to lead
  - don't use it TOO EARLY (before you 'unblock' another suit) [p107]
 - 'plan the play'
■ overtaking (play card fr 1 hand ^ 1 already played fr opp hand)
& underplaying (play high card fr BOTH hands but lower in 2nd) [p120]
 - done to unblock suit (manage entries)
 - duck - intentional decline win (safe w/ stoppers in ev suit))
 - overtake sacrifices high card to > entry to other hand
 - underplay sacrifices high card to ENSURE lead stays in SAME hand
Establishment & Promotion (take tricks w/ lower cards)

Basic Constructive Bidding

Intro
Standard American vs 2/1 ('two-over-one' game forcing) systems.
constructive (stong 1-side-only) vs competative bidding.
Auction
■ dealer acts 1st; 
■ bids must increase
■ bids sort by level & then strain (suits in alpha order > NT) [p18]
 NT  ♠  ♥  ♦  ♣
    S   H   D   C
■ last bid > declaring side (declarer = 1st to name strain)
■ 'opening leader' = person to L of 'declarer'
■ 1♠ = have at least 5 ♠ & '>avg hand' (in 2/1)
■ partner bidding in same suit = have at least 3 of that suit
■ 'agreements' (artificial bids; not natural) vs 'rules'
 e.g. you & partner 'agree' 1♥ open = >5♦
Goals
? best combined (w/ partner) suit + # tricks can take.
MAJOR (♠♥) & MINOR (♦♣) suits:
●'Game' contract: 3NT, 4 major; 5 minor
'partscore'/'partial': below ^
●1st goal: ? can play w/ major trump suit. If not - ? play NT contact (req 9 tricks). Last resort = play in minor suit.
●After find best suit > ? combined strength
Want to bid 'game' if can make it!
SLAMS: small = 12 tricks; grand = 13;

Opening Bid
■ Opening 1 of suit 'promises' 12 High Card Points (HCP):
   A=4 | K=3 | Q=2 | J=1
■ If can open (^) - ? which suit:
● 1) 1♥ or 1♠ promise 5 cards (2/1 = '5-card majors' system).
● 2) open longest suit.
● - val for prtnr to know if have 4 or 5-card major. OPEN of 1♥ or 1♠ = 5 (later = 4).
■ w/ long suits of = lgth [p65]:
● 1) open higher ranked (not stronger).
● 2) w/ 2 3-card minors > ♣ (1♦ open = at least 4 ♦).
■ 1&2NT (prefer over 1 of suit):
● Balanced hand: no sigletons or voids & only 1 doubleton (4333; 4432; 5332).
● 1NT = 15-17 HCP + balanced.
● 2NT = 20-21 HCP + balanced.

Responding to 1 of Suit
■ PRIORITIES:
● Support opener's MAJOR
● Suggest new suit (looking for a fit)
● Suggest NT if no viable MAJOR fit
● Support opener's minor ✍
■ Communicate hand strength (req 25 HCP to bid game):
● 6-9 HCP (min)
 - pass w/ less
● 10-12 HCP (invitational; inv)
● 13+ HCP (game forcing; GF)
■ Major suit fit = 8 cards bet 2 hands
- w/ open of 1 ♥ or ♠ = 5 cards > respr can raise w/ 3
● fit w/ 'min' > raise 1 ✍
● fit w/ 'inv' > raise 2
● fit w/ 'forcing' > bid new suit 1st
■ Bidding major locates major fit. Bidding minor > ? play NT contract (strgth in q suit).
● Bid new suit (promise 4 cards except 2♥ resp to 1♠ = 5♥) if can't raise opener's major or opener bids minor!
 1-level new = 6+ HCP (min hand).
 2-level (if 1-level unavail) new = 13+
  HCP (game forcing)  - but 'bid
  cheaply'!
2/1 = 5 card majors (15-17 HCP 1 NT open) + game forcing 2-level new suit resp.
■ Bid cheaply: don't 'jump' to 2 just bec have 13+ HCP. ✍
w/ mult poss new suits:
● start w/ longest ✍
● w/ 2 equal
  - 5 cards: start higher
  - 4 cards: bid lower ✏
 w/ no 4-card suit to bid & min or inv hand bid 1NT ✏
■ Responders new suit bid is forcing (prtnr must bid again if interv opp passes).
1 NT resp is special:
 † post minor open 1NT = min hnd & is not forcing
 † post major opn 1NT = min or inv & is forcing

● if rspr > single raise:
 ∆ min hd - pass

 ∆ min hd - pass or ^ to gm (or > 3NT)
 ∆ stonger hd - ^ to gm (or > 3NT)
